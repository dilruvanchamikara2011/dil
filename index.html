📊 Data & Information

📥 What is Data?

Data are raw material about numbers, letters, images, sounds, symbols, people, place, business transactions, etc.

They are unprocessed things and don't have any meaning.

✨ Examples of Data:
  • Numbers of the student in a class
  • Name of the person
  • Age
  • Total population of a country
  • Student's Marks for a subject

📤 What is Information?

Information can be obtained when data are processed in a meaningful way.

Information is processed data.

✨ Examples of Information:
  • Rank of a student
  • NIC No
  • Weather report
  • Average marks of the students for a subject

💻 What is ICT?

🖥️

ICT stands for Information and Communication Technology.

It is the combination of Information Technology (IT) and Communication Technology (CT).

💾 Information Technology (IT)

The various technology which is used to converting data into information called Information Technology.

📌 Examples:
💻 Computers 📊 Software 🗄️ Databases 🌐 Networks

📡 Communication Technology (CT)

Once the information obtained that can be share with others that technology called communications technology

📌 Examples:
📱 Mobile phones 🌍 Internet 📧 Email 💬 Social Media
🔄 + 📡 = 💻

So the Information Communication Technology (ICT) is the various technology which is used to converting data into information as well as sharing them with the others.

🏥 ICT in Health

MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)

This machine can create digitalized images of internal organs of the body by using strong magnetic fields and radio waves.

Specially for soft tissue in brain, brain cells, blood cells.

It will produce details images of your internal organs

CAT - Computerized Axial Tomography Machine

Using this machine three dimensional (3D) images of different parts of the body can be made.

Mostly borne related injuries, cancer diagnosis

ECG - Electrocardiogram Machine

used to monitor the heart beat. And This machine records the electrical impulses.

Cardiac Screening Machine

This machine displays the physiology of the heart and it displays the movements inside the heart

EEG - (Electro-encephalography)

This machine is used to record the activities of the brain.

Blood Sugar Testing Machine

Blood Pressure Measuring Machine

If this device we can minimize the harmful effects caused by insects.

✅ Benefit: Therefore the harmfulness of insects can be reduced.

These devices control the supply of water as per the data fed.

✅ Benefit: Wastage of water and destruction of crops due to lack of water supply are minimized.

ICT is used to control light, moisture and air inside the greenhouse.

📜 Evolution of Computer

1642

➕ Adding Machine

Blaise Pascal invented a machine called the Adding Machine. This was the world's first ever mathematical machine.

Ancient

🧮 Abacus

1801

🃏 Punch Card System

A French Scientist named Joseph Jacquard invented a mechanical loom using Punch Card System.

⭐ Important

👨‍🔬 Charles Babbage - Father of Computing

Charles Babbage started to make his Analytical Engine using the Punch Card System concept. This machine was based on the concepts input, process, output and store. Since this concept helped in the development of the computer, Charles Babbage is called the father of computing.

👩‍💻 First Programmer

Ada Augusta Lovelace

Madam Ada Augusta Lovelace is considered as the first programmer.

1944

🔢 MARK 1

Howard Aiken invented the machine called Automatic Sequence Control Calculator. This was named MARK 1.

💡

Summary
From Abacus to MARK 1, the evolution of computers has been a journey of innovation. Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine introduced the concepts of Input, Process, Output, and Store which are still used in modern computers.

🖥️ Generations of Computer

📟 First Generation Computers (1940-1956)
🔧 Major Hardware Technology:
Vacuum Tubes used, Punch Cards are used for input, process, output and storage of data
💾 Software Used:
Machine language used to program, Assembly language, Stored Program Concept
⚡ Characteristics:
High Heat Generation, Slow in processing, Large in size, Not Portable, Consumes a lot of electricity, Expensive
🖥️ Systems invented:
NIAC, EDVAC, EDSAC, UNIVAC, IBM 701
🔬 Second Generation (1956-1963)
🔧 Major Hardware Technology:
Transistors is technology, Punch card were used, Floppy Disk, magnetic disk for Storage, Tape for Secondary Storage
💾 Software Used:
High-level Programming language, Use of Assembly Language
⚡ Characteristics:
Less heat Generation, Low power consumption, Comparatively faster than the first generation
🖥️ Systems:
well 400, IBM 7030, CDC 1604, UNIVAC LARC, Honey
🔌 Third Generation Computers (1964-1975)
🔧 Major Hardware Technology:
Integrated Circuits (IC), High capacity disks for secondary storage, Keyboard and mouse for data input
💾 Software Used:
birth of Operating Systems (OS), Well developed Programming languages, high level computer languages for coding
⚡ Characteristics:
smaller in size, Less heat Generation, Comparatively faster than the second generation, Expensive, Low power consumption
🖥️ Systems:
IBM-360, IBM-370, PDP-8, PDP-11, CDC 6600
💻 Fourth Generation Computers (1975-1989)
🔧 Major Hardware Technology:
LSIC (Large Scale Integrated Circuits) and VLSIC (Very Large Scale Integrated Circuits), Microprocessor, Palm Tops, High Capacity hard disks, Floppy disk, Optical disk, Personal computers (PC), Faster computer networks
💾 Software Used:
OS with GUI (Graphical User Interface), UNIX OS
⚡ Characteristics:
Very small in size, Portable, Upgradable
🖥️ Systems:
IBM PC, Apple
🤖 Fifth Generation Computers (1989 to present)
🔧 Major Hardware Technology:
ULSI (Ultra Large Scale Integration), Very High Capacity Hard disks and optical disks, Internet
💾 Software Used:
Operating Systems with GUI, Internet and multimedia applications, Voice recognition based on AI, character recognition, Hand-writing recognition systems
⚡ Characteristics:
Portable, Less Expensive, Smaller in size, Easy operation, High reliability, High efficiency
🖥️ Systems:
IBM notebooks, Pentium PCs, SUN workstation
